*Theory Package: capital_flows*

## generator_guidance

### When Phase A (Accumulation) is Active

Generate hypotheses about:

1. **The specific PE gap.** State the EM PE, DM PE, and the discount percentage. Compare to historical episodes: is the current gap wider or narrower than the pre-2002–2007 gap? How does it compare to the pre-2009 gap? Wider gap = larger potential magnitude of rotation.

2. **Why the catalysts are absent.** Name the specific reasons rotation isn't happening yet: dollar is at X level and rising/flat because Y, China credit impulse is negative because Z, geopolitical risk is elevated because W. Each absent catalyst has different implications for WHEN it might arrive.

3. **India as independent play.** During Accumulation, INDA may work independently of the broader EM thesis. Generate India-specific hypotheses separately from the EEM/FXI thesis. India's domestic demand story provides an independent case that doesn't require dollar weakness or China recovery.

4. **Watchlist levels.** Specify the trigger levels that would shift from Accumulation to Rotation: DXY below X, USD/CNY below Y, China credit impulse above Z, FXI above W. These give the system concrete thresholds to monitor.

### When Phase B (Rotation) is Active

Generate hypotheses about:

1. **Regional sequencing and sizing.** Which EM markets first? What percentage allocation? China leads → broad EM follows → derivative plays (VGK, EWJ). Sizing should reflect conviction levels (see TACTICAL.md for suggested ranges).

2. **Duration of the rotation.** Is this a 2-year or 5-year rotation? The credit impulse cycle and dollar cycle determine duration. If China credit impulse is likely to be positive for only 12–18 months, the rotation is shorter. If fiscal dominance is structurally weakening the dollar, the rotation is longer.

3. **What would kill the rotation.** Trade war escalation, China credit impulse reversal, dollar re-strengthening, geopolitical crisis. State the kill conditions explicitly so the system knows when to exit.

### When Adjacent (Rotation) with Active (Accumulation)

This is the highest-value state. Generate PAIRED hypotheses:

- **"Rotation confirms" hypothesis:** What the portfolio should look like if the emerging catalysts strengthen. Sizing, sequencing, duration.
- **"False start" hypothesis:** What the portfolio should look like if the catalysts fade. Specifically: which positions to cut first, what level of reversal constitutes failure.

## generator_prohibitions

1. **Do NOT claim EM will outperform just because it's cheap.** Cheapness without catalyst = value trap. The 1997–1998 Asian crisis shows that EM can be cheap and get destroyed. ALWAYS require a catalyst (dollar weakening, China easing) before generating rotation hypotheses.

2. **Do NOT treat all EM as homogeneous.** China, India, Brazil, Korea, Taiwan are different economies with different drivers. A China credit impulse helps China and commodity EM but may not help India. The generator must specify WHICH EM markets and WHY.

3. **Do NOT ignore the geopolitical overlay.** The 2017–2018 experience shows trade wars can abort a rotation with strong fundamental support. Every rotation hypothesis should include a geopolitical risk assessment, even if it concludes "risk is manageable."

4. **Do NOT assume rotation means US decline.** During 2003–2007, SPY returned +12% annualised. EM simply returned +35%. Frame rotation as "add EM, reduce US overweight" not "sell US, buy EM."

5. **Do NOT generate Phase B (Rotation) hypotheses when only Phase A (Accumulation) is Active.** The temptation is to anticipate the rotation before catalysts confirm. Accumulation hypotheses are about monitoring and watchlist-building, not deployment.

6. **Do NOT claim dollar direction without citing the causal explanation.** Dollar direction is exogenous to this theory. If the generator predicts dollar weakness, it must cite the source theory (fiscal dominance, debt cycle) providing the causal mechanism.

## evaluator_priority_checks

1. **Phase classification accuracy.** Is the system genuinely in Rotation (catalysts confirmed) or still in Accumulation (catalysts absent)? Check DURATION requirements: dollar weakening for 3+ months, credit impulse positive for 3+ months, EEM outperforming for 3+ months. Premature Rotation calls lead to value trap losses.

2. **China risk assessment.** Every FXI/KWEB hypothesis must address: is China in a Japan-style balance sheet recession? If yes, the credit impulse catalyst is impotent even if formally positive. Require specific evidence on housing starts, property prices, bank NPLs, and credit transmission — not just the headline credit impulse number.

3. **Dollar direction credibility.** Dollar direction is the binding constraint. If the generator predicts rotation while the dollar is strengthening, challenge it. The generator must explain WHY the dollar will weaken (fiscal dominance, relative growth shift, policy divergence). "Dollar should weaken because it's overvalued" is insufficient — the dollar can stay overvalued for years.

4. **Magnitude realism.** FXI +50% in year one is historically achievable during strong rotation (2009, 2006–2007). But it requires confirmed catalysts, not Accumulation-phase positioning. If the generator claims +50% FXI during Accumulation, that's an error — Accumulation produces +/-10% with no clear direction.

5. **India independence check.** India has its own cycle and can work during Accumulation. A hypothesis that lumps India with broad EM ("EEM will outperform") may miss the India-specific opportunity. Check whether INDA deserves its own hypothesis independent of the FXI/EEM thesis.

## evaluator_rejection_criteria

Reject hypotheses that:

1. **Invoke Phase B language during Phase A.** Any hypothesis that says "deploy to EM" or "rotate now" when only Accumulation indicators are Active is a phase mismatch error.

2. **Predict EM outperformance without a catalyst.** "EM is cheap therefore it will outperform" violates the theory's core requirement that cheapness PLUS catalyst is needed.

3. **Claim dollar direction without sourcing the mechanism.** Dollar direction is exogenous. The hypothesis must cite which theory (fiscal dominance, debt cycle) provides the dollar-weakening mechanism.

4. **Treat EM as a single asset class.** "Buy EM" without specifying regional composition and sequencing is insufficiently specific. Which EM and why?

5. **Omit geopolitical risk assessment for any China hypothesis.** FXI/KWEB hypotheses without a geopolitical risk section should be returned for revision.

6. **Assign magnitudes inconsistent with the phase.** Accumulation-phase magnitude estimates should be modest (+/-10% direction-ambiguous). Rotation-phase estimates can be aggressive (+30–50% for FXI). Cross-phase magnitude errors indicate the generator doesn't understand the phase distinction.

## composition_rules

### High-Value Compositions

| Composition | Why It Works | What It Adds |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| capital_flows + structural_fragility (Resolving) | A US fragility break is the primary catalyst for converting Accumulation to Rotation. The US premium evaporates during the break. Tradeable sequencing: sell together → EM recovers faster → relative rotation begins. | Provides the TRIGGER. Specificity: buy EM AFTER the initial correlated decline, not before. |
| capital_flows + valuation_mean_reversion | US expensive + EM cheap = fund rotation by reducing US. Two independent theories pointing to the same portfolio action (underweight US, overweight EM). | Aligns two independent theories into a single portfolio expression. Higher confidence than either alone because the case is supported by BOTH domestic valuation and international flow arguments. |
| capital_flows + fiscal_dominance_liquidity | Fiscal dominance → dollar weakness → EM outperformance. Fiscal dominance provides the structural dollar-weakening catalyst that capital flows needs. | Distinguishes between slow rotation (fiscal-dominance-driven, +20–30% FXI over 12–18 months) and fast rotation (fragility-break-driven, +40–50% FXI in 6 months). Sizing and urgency differ. |
| capital_flows + debt_cycle_short (US Contraction, EM Expansion) | Maximum rotation force. US recession + EM growth = widest possible growth differential. | Critical conditionality: requires EM to be RECEIVING capital, not just US losing it. Global recession does NOT produce rotation. |
| capital_flows + monetary_architecture | Structural dollar weakness from reserve diversification supports the rotation thesis on a multi-decade horizon. | Extends rotation thesis from cyclical (2–5 years) to structural (decade+). The "no alternative to dollar" argument weakens as collateral substitution proceeds. |

### Low-Value or Prohibited Compositions

| Composition | Why It Doesn't Work |
|-------------|---------------------|
| capital_flows + debt_cycle_short (Global Contraction) | If EM is also contracting, no rotation occurs. Global recession sends capital to safe havens (dollar, Treasuries), not to EM. The composition must verify EM is expanding before claiming rotation. |
| capital_flows + structural_fragility (Building) without a catalyst | Fragility building doesn't trigger rotation — only the BREAK does. During the building phase, US premium persists and may even increase. |

### Composition Discipline

- All pairwise interactions are authoritative in INTERACTION_MATRIX.md. Refer there for invariant logic.
- The most common failure mode in composition is assuming that "multiple theories Active" means "higher conviction in all predictions." It does not. Some compositions CREATE contradictions (e.g., fiscal dominance may prevent the fragility break that would trigger rotation). The generator must identify whether the theories reinforce or contradict in the current state.
- For the shared-upstream-cause concern flagged by the user: capital_flows' rotation thesis shares the "dollar weakening" driver with fiscal dominance theories. When both are Active, this is the same force observed from two angles, not two independent forces. Conviction in dollar weakness should not be double-counted.

## common_failure_modes

### Generator Failures

1. **Premature rotation call.** The generator declares Rotation based on one month of data (one positive China PMI print, one month of dollar weakness). Duration thresholds exist for a reason: 3+ months of confirmed catalyst. One-month signals frequently reverse.

2. **Homogeneous EM treatment.** Treating EEM as a monolithic asset when China, India, and commodity EM have different drivers and different timelines. The generator should produce China-specific, India-specific, and broad-EM hypotheses separately.

3. **Ignoring the value trap risk.** During Accumulation, the generator may be tempted to start positioning for rotation "early." The historical record shows that Accumulation can persist for 3–7 years. Early positioning in EM during Accumulation destroys carry.

4. **Dollar direction assertion without mechanism.** Claiming "the dollar will weaken" without citing the causal theory (fiscal dominance, relative growth). Dollar direction is the binding constraint and cannot be asserted without source.

### Evaluator Failures

1. **Over-crediting cheapness.** Accepting "EM is cheap" as sufficient evidence for rotation. Cheapness is necessary but not sufficient — the catalyst requirement is load-bearing.

2. **Under-weighting China risk.** Treating China credit impulse as a reliable mechanical signal when the balance sheet recession context flag (see ACTIVATION.md) may render the signal impotent. The evaluator should require evidence that credit is TRANSMITTING, not just that the impulse number is positive.

3. **Missing the India independence opportunity.** Rejecting India hypotheses because "broad EM rotation hasn't started." India can work independently — rejecting INDA hypotheses during Accumulation because EEM isn't working is an evaluator error.

4. **Failing to check composition conditionality.** The debt_cycle_short × capital_flows composition requires EM expansion + US contraction. Accepting "US recession → buy EM" without verifying EM is actually expanding is a composition failure.
