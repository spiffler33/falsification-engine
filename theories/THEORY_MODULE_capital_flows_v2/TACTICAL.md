## regional_sequencing

The sequencing of returns during a confirmed rotation follows a historically consistent pattern:

1. **China first (Month 0–6).** FXI and KWEB move first and most aggressively. In every historical rotation, Chinese equities led. Suggested allocation: 20–30% of EM allocation to China.

2. **Broad EM follows (Month 3–12).** EEM follows with a 2–6 month lag. The lag exists because broad EM includes non-China markets that react to second-order effects (commodity prices, supply chain activity). Suggested allocation: 30–40% of EM allocation.

3. **India on its own cycle (throughout).** INDA participates in rotation but has an independent domestic demand story. Can outperform during Accumulation when EEM doesn't, and during late Rotation when China risks resurface. Suggested allocation: 15–25% of EM allocation.

4. **Europe as derivative (Month 6–18).** VGK benefits from China revenue exposure. Lags the direct EM plays. Useful for investors wanting EM exposure with DM governance. Suggested allocation: 10–15% of EM allocation.

5. **Japan complicated (Month 6–18).** EWJ benefits from Asian growth but yen dynamics may offset returns. Lower conviction. Suggested allocation: 5–10% of EM allocation, consider currency-hedged (DXJ).

## current_theme_specifics

*This section is explicitly ephemeral. It captures implementation details tied to the current macro moment. Its presence is never required for CORE.md to remain valid.*

**Current state (as of early 2026):** Phase A (Accumulation) is the operative state. The valuation setup is extreme (EM at ~11–12x PE vs. US at ~22–24x PE, a 45–50% discount). The catalysts are absent: dollar remains strong/sideways, China credit impulse has been flat to negative amid the property sector downturn, and geopolitical risk (US-China competition, Taiwan) is elevated but not at crisis level.

**India exception.** INDA has been working independently of the broad EM rotation thesis, driven by domestic demand, demographic dividend, and structural reform (UPI, GST, manufacturing push). India-specific hypotheses should be generated separately from the FXI/EEM thesis.

**China property overhang.** The primary risk to the credit impulse catalyst is that China's property sector downturn has structural characteristics (developer defaults, housing price declines, household balance sheet damage) that may impair credit transmission even when the impulse turns positive. This is the Japan-style balance sheet recession risk flagged in the context flags. The generator should require specific evidence on housing starts, property prices, and bank NPLs before generating high-conviction FXI/KWEB hypotheses.

**Trade policy uncertainty.** Tariff levels and supply chain reshoring trends remain in flux. The generator should monitor tariff schedules and manufacturing PMI data for evidence that trade policy is structurally impairing EM earnings potential (soft falsifier S2).
