## sector_depth

The long-term debt cycle operates at the macro level. Sector implications flow primarily through the short-term cycle module and structural fragility module. Direct late-cycle sector effects:

- **Financials:** Net interest margin dynamics change in the late cycle — banks become structurally dependent on Fed facilities, yield curve management becomes policy-driven rather than market-driven. Financial repression compresses bank profitability over the long term.
- **Real assets (energy, materials, real estate):** Benefit from the inflationary resolution path (step 7a). If financial repression is the resolution, real assets outperform financial assets structurally. Energy and materials at low S&P 500 weights are late-cycle value opportunities.
- **Technology:** Benefits from nominal growth but faces multiple compression in an inflationary environment. The tension: tech earnings grow nominally but the PE applied to those earnings declines as inflation and real rates create valuation headwinds.

## regional_sequencing

The late long-term cycle is most advanced in different economies, creating relative-value implications:

| Region | Cycle Stage | Implication |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Japan | Late stage since early 1990s (~30+ years). Most advanced. | Template for the stagnation resolution path (soft falsifier S2). Japan's policy evolution (QE → ZIRP → NIRP → YCC → coordinated fiscal-monetary) is the roadmap for where the US cycle may go if the Japan path materialises. |
| United States | Late stage since ~2008. Primary subject of this theory. | MP2-to-MP3 transition. Currently the most important single-country application. |
| Europe | Intermediate. ECB deployed MP2 (2015-2022) but fiscal coordination (MP3) is constrained by EU treaty structure. | May follow the US path with a 5-10 year lag. The fiscal constraint (EU budget rules, no fiscal union) could produce a different resolution channel — more Japan-like for the periphery, more inflationary for the core. |
| EM countries | Generally earlier in their long-term cycles (lower debt/GDP). | Subject to different dynamics. EM long-cycle risk concentrates in countries that have taken on dollar-denominated debt (foreign-currency mismatch) — their late-cycle dynamics play out through external crises rather than internal MP escalation. |

## current_theme_specifics

*This section is explicitly ephemeral. It describes the current macro moment within the long-term cycle framework. Update as conditions change. Its absence must never invalidate CORE.md.*

**Current MP classification: MP2-to-MP3 transition.**

- **MP1** is nominally available (rates ~5%) but the speed of return to ZLB in recent crises (2020: zero within 2 weeks) means MP1's capacity is borrowed time. One recession and rates return to zero.
- **MP2** has been substantially deployed (Fed BS ~$7.4T, ~26% of GDP). QT is attempting to unwind, but the March 2023 SVB episode showed that QT can be reversed within days when financial stability demands it. The balance sheet is structurally larger than pre-2008 and cannot return to those levels.
- **MP3** is operative. Fiscal deficits of $2T+ during non-recession ARE fiscal-monetary coordination, whether labelled as such or not. The Fed's inability to produce recession through rate hikes (because fiscal spending offsets monetary tightening) is empirical proof that MP3 is active.

**Crisis sequencing template (late long-term cycle):**

When a recession or financial stress event occurs in the late long-term cycle, the predicted sequencing is:
- Phase 1 (panic, weeks 1-8): risk-off, TLT rallies +15% to +25%, gold flat-to-down
- Phase 2 (policy response, months 3-12): rotate from TLT to GLD/TIP/equities as MP3 fiscal response generates inflation expectations
- Phase 3 (recovery/reflation, months 12-24): overweight equities and commodities, underweight bonds

This sequencing is specific to the late long-term cycle. In the early cycle, Phase 1 can last years because the policy response is smaller and deflationary pressures dominate.
