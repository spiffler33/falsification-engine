## directional_predictions

These predictions apply when the theory is Active. All timeframes are rolling multi-year — this is a structural positioning theory, not a tactical trading signal.

| Asset | Direction | Magnitude Range | Timeframe | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| GLD | Structural outperformance | +8% to +20% annualized real | Rolling 3–5 years | Gold is the primary beneficiary of devaluation arithmetic. Three reinforcing channels: (1) inflation hedge as dollar purchasing power erodes, (2) reserve asset substitution as central banks diversify from Treasuries (structural floor under price — mechanism owned by `monetary_architecture`, see INTERACTION_MATRIX.md), (3) monetary premium expansion as the gold/oil ratio rises above historical norms. |
| SLV | Outperform, lagging gold | +5% to +15% annualized | Rolling 3–5 years | Silver benefits from the same monetary premium channel as gold but with higher volatility and additional industrial demand (electrification, solar). Silver underperforms gold during early phase of devaluation recognition, outperforms during acceleration phase (higher beta). Gold/silver ratio above 80 = silver relatively cheap within the complex. |
| TLT | Structural underperformance in real terms | -2% to -8% annualized real return | Rolling 3–5 years | Long-duration nominal bonds are the primary VICTIM of devaluation. The coupon is fixed in nominal terms — inflation erodes real value of both coupon and principal. Additionally: rising term premium (market demanding compensation for duration in uncertain fiscal environment) pressures prices independently of rate moves. TLT can deliver positive nominal returns if rates fall — but real returns are still poor if inflation persists above the coupon rate. |
| TIP | Outperform TLT | +3% to +10% annualized relative | Rolling 3–5 years | TIPS protect principal against CPI inflation. In a devaluation regime, TIPS preserve real value while nominal bonds lose it. The breakeven spread (TIPS yield vs. nominal yield) should widen as the market prices in sustained higher inflation. TIPS are the bond market's expression of the devaluation thesis. |
| DBC | Outperform nominal bonds | +5% to +15% annualized | Rolling 3–5 years | Commodities benefit from both the monetary premium channel (real assets hold value during devaluation) and the demand channel (fiscal spending on infrastructure, defense, energy subsidies supports consumption). Commodities are noisier than gold (subject to supply/demand cycles) but directionally aligned. |
| UUP (Dollar) | Structural decline | -2% to -5% annualized on trade-weighted basis | Rolling 3–5 years | Dollar weakens structurally because: (a) fiscal profligacy reduces confidence in dollar-denominated claims, (b) non-dollar energy settlement reduces structural dollar demand (mechanism owned by `monetary_architecture`), (c) central bank reserve diversification from Treasuries to gold reduces official dollar holdings. The decline is SLOW — not a crisis, not a collapse, but a persistent grind. The dollar can rally sharply on short-term dynamics (flight to safety, rate differentials) within the structural decline. |
| SPY | Mediocre real returns, positive nominal | +3% to +8% annualized nominal, 0% to +3% real | Rolling 3–5 years | Equities are mixed in a devaluation regime. Nominal earnings rise with inflation (revenue adjusts upward). But multiples compress because real discount rates are uncertain and inflation compresses PE ratios. Net: nominal returns positive, real returns mediocre. Equities with pricing power and tangible assets outperform. Long-duration growth equities underperform on a real basis. |
| IBIT | Outperform if adoption thesis holds | +15% to +40% annualized with ±30% drawdowns | Rolling 3–5 years | Bitcoin as devaluation hedge — fixed supply, outside government control, increasingly held by institutions. The thesis is contested: Bitcoin has NOT proven itself through a sustained devaluation regime. Its correlation with risk assets during 2022 tightening weakened the "inflation hedge" narrative. Include with lower conviction than GLD. |

## regional_sequencing

Not directly applicable. The theory is US-centric by construction (US fiscal arithmetic). International implications flow through dollar weakness:

- **EM outperformance:** Dollar weakness is a tailwind for EM equities and EM local-currency debt. This channel is owned by `capital_flows` and the fiscal_dominance_liquidity × capital_flows interaction in INTERACTION_MATRIX.md.
- **Developed market divergence:** Europe and Japan have their own fiscal arithmetic — their trajectories may differ. This theory does not predict relative performance across developed markets.

### The Arithmetic in Numbers (as of early 2026)

| Item | Approximate Value | Trajectory |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| Total federal debt | ~$35.5T | Growing ~$2T/year |
| Annual interest expense | ~$1.1T | Rising as low-rate debt rolls |
| Annual tax receipts | ~$4.8T | Growing ~3–4%/year nominally |
| Interest / receipts | ~23% | Rising — the critical ratio |
| Defense spending | ~$886B | Growing ~3%/year |
| Social Security + Medicare | ~$2.4T | Growing ~5–6%/year (demographics) |
| Structural deficit | ~$2.0T | Cannot shrink without policy change |
| Weighted avg. interest rate on debt | ~3.3% | Rising toward market rates (~4.5%) |
| Implied interest expense at full rollover | ~$1.6T | If all debt repriced to current rates |

The last row is the delayed fuse: when all existing debt has rolled into current market rates, interest expense reaches approximately $1.6T — roughly 33% of current receipts. This is the arithmetic that makes the trajectory untenable even without additional borrowing.

### Current Market Context

- Interest expense crossed $1T annualized in 2024, surpassing defense spending. The next political threshold (interest exceeding Medicare or Social Security) would represent a genuine fiscal crisis point.
- Gold has moved from ~$1,800 (2022) to $2,800+ (2025–2026), partially pre-pricing the devaluation thesis. The question of how much is already discounted determines the incremental opportunity.
- Both major parties propose fiscal expansion as of the current political cycle. No credible deficit reduction framework exists (context flag in ACTIVATION.md).

## expression_monitors

These are NOT theory falsifiers — they monitor whether the trade is working, not whether the theory is true. If an expression monitor triggers, the correct response is to re-examine the trade vehicle, not to abandon the fiscal arithmetic diagnosis.

| ID | Condition | Metric | Threshold | What It Tests |
|----|-----------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| EM1 | Gold underperforms SHY for 5+ consecutive years while arithmetic indicators are triggered | GLD total return vs. SHY total return | GLD trails SHY for 5+ consecutive calendar years during a period when interest/receipts is above 20% | Tests whether GLD is the right implementation of the thesis, not whether the arithmetic is real. The arithmetic can be untenable AND gold can still be the wrong vehicle — because the dollar retains reserve status longer than expected, because real rates stay positive and punish zero-yield assets, or because devaluation transmits through channels gold doesn't capture. If triggered: re-examine expression (TIPS, commodities, pricing-power equities may be better vehicles). Do not automatically abandon the fiscal arithmetic diagnosis. However: if the theory cannot produce a working trade, its utility for portfolio construction is zero regardless of intellectual validity. |
| EM2 | TIP fails to outperform TLT over 3+ years while inflation is above 2.5% | TIP total return vs. TLT total return | TIP trails TLT for 3+ consecutive calendar years while CPI YoY is above 2.5% | Tests whether the TIPS breakeven trade captures the devaluation. If inflation is running above 2.5% and TIPS still underperform nominal Treasuries, the breakeven market is mispricing or the devaluation is transmitting through non-CPI channels (asset inflation, currency depreciation rather than consumer prices). |
