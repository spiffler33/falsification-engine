## generator_guidance

When this theory is Active, generate hypotheses about:

**1. The current pace of the transition.** State the latest central bank gold buying data (tonnes/year), the foreign official Treasury holdings share, the gold/oil ratio, and the RMB share of global payments. These are the EMPIRICAL anchors — every claim about the transition should be grounded in the latest data from ACTIVATION.md indicators. If buying has accelerated (above 1,000 tonnes), the transition is on track or ahead. If it has slowed (below 600 tonnes), the urgency may be fading (state falsifier territory).

**2. Recent plumbing stress events.** Check the "plumbing state" context flag in ACTIVATION.md. If stressed: identify the specific event (basis widening, repo spike, new facility), state the Fed response, and apply the post-intervention tactical pattern from TACTICAL.md. If calm for 2+ years: note this as H4 potentially approaching — the "system stabilizing" falsifier gains evidence.

**3. The gold floor estimate.** Given the current CB buying pace and gold price, estimate the price level below which central bank buying absorbs available supply. At 1,000 tonnes/year buying and ~3,500 tonnes/year mine supply, central banks absorb ~30% of new supply. The floor estimate should be updated with each run based on latest buying and price data.

**4. Interaction with fiscal dominance.** If `fiscal_dominance_arithmetic` is also Active, state the double-sourced demand thesis explicitly: domestic debasement hedgers + foreign CB reserve managers = two independent buyer pools. Reference INTERACTION_MATRIX.md for the pairwise logic and the Shared Upstream Cause Warning. The two demand sources strengthen the floor prediction but should NOT be treated as independent confirmation — see composition_rules below.

**5. Interaction with structural fragility.** If `structural_fragility` is Active (Building), a plumbing stress event or financial crisis is a BUYING OPPORTUNITY for gold, not a selling event. The generator should frame the post-intervention pattern: initial dip → Fed intervention → gold recovery within 3–6 months. Reference the tactical template in TACTICAL.md.

## generator_prohibitions

1. **Do NOT predict dollar collapse or loss of reserve status.** The theory predicts a GRADUAL transition to a multipolar system. The dollar remains the dominant reserve throughout. Hyperbolic predictions undermine the thesis's credibility.

2. **Do NOT claim precise timing.** "Gold's reserve share will reach 15% by 2030" is overspecified. "Gold's reserve share is trending from ~10% toward 15–20% over the next 10–20 years" is appropriately vague on timing.

3. **Do NOT confuse this with a simple gold bull thesis.** Many things drive gold prices (inflation expectations, real rates, risk sentiment, jewelry demand). This theory identifies ONE specific driver: the monetary premium from collateral regime transition. The generator must distinguish the monetary architecture channel from other gold drivers.

4. **Do NOT treat plumbing stress as systemic risk TO the thesis.** Plumbing stress is EVIDENCE FOR the thesis. The generator should frame stress events as confirming data points and tactical buying opportunities, not as threats to the gold position (except for the brief initial dip during acute dollar-squeeze phases).

5. **Do NOT claim precise magnitude without acknowledging sample-size uncertainty.** Monetary system transitions occur once or twice per century. Any precise magnitude claim should be flagged for N≈2 uncertainty. Direction has higher confidence than magnitude.

6. **Do NOT conflate this theory's mechanisms with fiscal_dominance_arithmetic.** Both theories support gold, but through different channels. Monetary_architecture owns the reserve-composition / collateral-substitution channel. Fiscal_dominance_arithmetic owns the domestic devaluation / nominal-claims-impairment channel. The generator must keep the channels separate even when the conclusion (long gold) is the same.

## evaluator_priority_checks

1. **Is the generator grounding claims in data?** CB buying tonnage, foreign Treasury holdings share, gold/oil ratio, basis swap levels — these are the empirical anchors from ACTIVATION.md. Reject any hypothesis that claims "the monetary system is transitioning" without citing the specific evidence. The thesis is falsifiable only if the generator states what the evidence IS.

2. **Is the generator distinguishing the monetary premium from other gold drivers?** If the hypothesis says "gold will rally because of the architecture transition" but gold is actually rallying because of CPI inflation fears, the generator is attributing the wrong cause. This matters for CONVICTION: if gold rallies for cyclical reasons and those reasons fade, gold may correct. If it rallies because of the structural transition, the rally is more durable.

3. **Is the generator handling plumbing events correctly?** During acute plumbing stress, gold may INITIALLY DECLINE (dollar squeeze, margin calls, forced liquidation). The generator should NOT interpret this as thesis failure. The thesis predicts: initial dip → Fed intervention → gold recovery within 3–6 months. If the generator panics during the dip and calls the thesis falsified, it misunderstands the mechanism. Enforce the 3–6 month evaluation window for plumbing events.

4. **Is the pace claim calibrated?** The transition takes decades. If the generator claims "gold reaches $5,000 in 18 months because of the architecture transition," the timeframe is inconsistent with the mechanism. The architecture transition adds +5% to +10% per year to gold's structural bid. Parabolic gold moves come from OTHER theories (fiscal dominance panic, currency crisis, systemic fragility break). This theory provides the FLOOR and the TREND, not the spike.

5. **Is the generator honest about sample size?** Any precise magnitude claim should be flagged for N≈2 uncertainty. The theory's direction (gold structurally bid) has higher confidence than its magnitude (+8% to +15% annualized) because direction requires only that the transition is occurring (well-evidenced) while magnitude requires knowing the speed (poorly calibrated from historical precedent).

6. **Is the composition producing specificity gain?** The highest-value composition is `monetary_architecture + fiscal_dominance_arithmetic` → double-sourced structural gold bid with specific floor estimate. If the composition doesn't produce a MORE SPECIFIC prediction about gold's floor, trend, or post-event performance than this theory alone, it's not adding value. Reference INTERACTION_MATRIX.md to validate composition logic.

## evaluator_rejection_criteria

Reject a hypothesis invoking this theory if:

1. It predicts dollar collapse, rapid loss of reserve status, or a crisis-driven transition. The theory's mechanism is gradual.
2. It claims gold will rally without citing specific ACTIVATION.md indicator data as evidence.
3. It conflates the monetary premium channel with cyclical gold drivers (inflation, real rates, risk sentiment) without distinguishing the contribution of each.
4. It treats a plumbing stress event as thesis failure rather than as thesis confirmation with a tactical buying window.
5. It composes with another theory without producing a more specific or more confident prediction than either theory alone.
6. It claims precise magnitude or timing inconsistent with the multi-decade horizon and N≈2 sample size.
7. It treats simultaneous activation of monetary_architecture and fiscal_dominance_arithmetic as independent confirmation when both share upstream indicators — see Shared Upstream Cause Warning in INTERACTION_MATRIX.md.

## common_failure_modes

### Generator Failure Modes

1. **Gold-bug hyperbole.** The generator adopts a doom narrative — dollar collapse, end of US hegemony, return to the gold standard. The theory is more measured than this. It predicts a gradual, multi-decade rebalancing, not a crisis.

2. **Conflating channels.** The generator says "gold will rally because of fiscal dominance and monetary architecture" without distinguishing which channel contributes what. The two channels have different time horizons, different magnitudes, and different falsifiers. Conflating them produces an unfalsifiable blob.

3. **Ignoring the initial dip during plumbing stress.** The generator claims gold will rally DURING the acute phase of a plumbing event. It won't — the dollar squeeze produces an initial dip. The thesis predicts the POST-intervention rally, not the initial response.

4. **Precise magnitude claims.** "Gold will return +12% this year from the architecture transition." The precision is unjustified given N≈2. Better: "The structural trend supports +8% to +15% annualized, but year-to-year variance is high."

5. **Treating context flags as scored indicators.** The generator cites "sanctions are expanding" or "BRICS is building payment infrastructure" as if these are mechanically scored. They are qualitative context flags — they inform the narrative but should not be treated as equivalent to the scored activation indicators.

### Evaluator Failure Modes

1. **Over-penalizing for gold underperformance in a single year.** Annual gold underperformance is noise within a multi-decade structural trend. The evaluator should assess on a rolling 5-year basis (expression monitor EM1), not year-to-year.

2. **Treating the gold/oil ratio as independently confirming both monetary_architecture and fiscal_dominance_arithmetic.** The ratio appears in both theories' activation tables. It is one observation interpreted through two lenses, not two independent observations. The evaluator must apply the Shared Upstream Cause Warning.

3. **Rejecting the thesis because of a gold dip during plumbing stress.** The 3–6 month evaluation window exists precisely for this scenario. The evaluator must not assess the thesis's validity during the acute phase.

4. **Insufficient skepticism on sample size.** The evaluator should apply a higher uncertainty bar to magnitude claims than to direction claims. Direction (gold structurally bid by the transition) is well-evidenced. Magnitude (the specific annualized return range) is poorly calibrated from history.
