## regional_sequencing

Not strongly applicable — the collateral substitution is a global phenomenon, not a country-by-country rotation. However, two observations:

1. **Non-allied central banks lead:** China, India, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Brazil, South Africa, and ASEAN members have the strongest sanctions-diversification incentive. Their buying drives the first phase of the transition. Allied central banks may follow later as the transition becomes consensus.

2. **Commodity exporters benefit:** Countries whose exports are real assets (energy, metals, agriculture) gain relative advantage in a multipolar monetary system that values collateral over credit. This connects to the capital_flows interaction (see INTERACTION_MATRIX.md).

## sector_depth

### Gold Miners

The equity expression of the gold thesis. Sector-specific considerations:

- **All-in sustaining cost (AISC):** The critical metric. Miners with AISC substantially below the gold price have high operating leverage. At gold prices well above the industry average AISC, margins are exceptional.
- **Jurisdiction risk:** Miners operating in politically stable jurisdictions carry lower expropriation risk. Relevant because the thesis is about geopolitical risk to financial assets — physical assets in unstable jurisdictions carry analogous risk.
- **Reserve quality:** Miners with long reserve lives and low-cost deposits benefit most from a structural gold bull. Depleting reserves during a structural bull market is the worst outcome.
- **Capital discipline:** The 2011–2015 gold bear market punished miners who expanded aggressively at the peak. Current management teams are more capital-disciplined, which supports margins.

## current_theme_specifics

*This section is explicitly ephemeral. Its contents reflect the current macro moment and will need revision as conditions evolve. Its presence is never required for CORE.md to remain valid.*

### Post-2022 Sanctions Environment (current as of early 2026)

- Central bank gold buying has sustained above 1,000 tonnes/year in 2022 and 2023, potentially moderating slightly in 2024–2025 but remaining well above the pre-2022 baseline of 400–600 tonnes.
- The buying is concentrated among PBOC, RBI, MAS, NBP, TCMB, and Gulf sovereign wealth funds.
- The gold/oil ratio is elevated above 25, consistent with monetary premium expansion.
- US-China strategic competition continues to reinforce the diversification incentive.
- BRICS+ expansion has increased the institutional voice for non-dollar alternatives, though alternative payment infrastructure remains pre-operational at meaningful scale.

### Post-Plumbing-Event Tactical Pattern

The historical template for plumbing stress episodes: initial gold dip (dollar squeeze, margin calls, forced liquidation) → Fed intervention (swap lines, new facilities, reserve injection) → gold recovery to higher levels within 3–6 months.

- **2020 template:** Gold dipped from ~$1,680 to ~$1,470 in the March panic, then rallied to ~$2,070 by August as intervention flooded the system.
- **2023 template:** SVB crisis → BTFP creation → gold rallied from ~$1,820 to ~$2,050 over the following 6 months.

When the "plumbing state" context flag in ACTIVATION.md reads "stressed," this pattern becomes the primary tactical consideration. The post-intervention period is the highest-conviction buying window for gold within the structural trend.
